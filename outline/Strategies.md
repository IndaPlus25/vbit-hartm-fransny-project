2. General Strategy: See when a fairvalue-gaps (FVG) appears, use a 100 day SMA to detect the overall trend of the stock. If we are in an uptrend then we will only buy and in a downtrend we will only sell. All trades will be performed within a few hours of when the market has opened to ensure adequate volume.

![Fair Value Gap Strategy](./images/FairValueGap.png)

Buying and Stop-Loss: When buying it will look for a FVG which is formed idealy by three candles. A FVG indicates an imbalance between buyers and sellers. It is formed as shown in the image above when the low of the third candle is higher than the high of the first candle, if they overlap then it is not a valid FVG. We will wait for the price to retrace back into the FVG, then after the first bullish candle is formed we will enter the trade. The stop-loss will be placed just below the FVG and we will target 1.5x the risk.
